SEO has changed — here's what that means

Five years ago, SEO was largely about putting keywords on your pages and getting other websites to link to you. Those things still matter. But Google has gotten significantly smarter, and the tactics that used to work quickly can now get you penalised.

In 2025, Google's algorithm is focused on one thing above all else: is this page genuinely useful to the person who found it? That sounds simple but it has profound implications for how you should approach your website content.

The biggest shift: Google can now tell the difference between content written to rank and content written to genuinely help someone. It increasingly rewards the latter and ignores the former.

What actually works for small business websites in 2025

1. Answering real questions your customers ask

The most valuable SEO content you can create is content that answers the specific questions your potential customers type into Google. Not generic questions — the specific ones relevant to your business, location, and industry.

A plumber in Brisbane should have pages that answer: "how much does it cost to replace a hot water system in Brisbane?" A marketing consultant in Sydney should have content answering: "do I need a marketing agency or a freelancer?" These aren't just blog topics — they're the exact queries your potential clients are already searching.

2. Technical basics done properly

Before you write a single word of content, make sure the technical foundations are right. Your site needs to load fast on mobile (under 3 seconds), have no broken links, use HTTPS, and have proper page titles and meta descriptions on every page.

3. Local SEO signals

If your business serves a specific city or region, local SEO is your highest-leverage opportunity. This means: a Google Business Profile that's fully filled out and actively managed, consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) information across the web, and content that specifically mentions your location and the locations you serve.

Quick wins you can do this week

  • Check your page titles — every page on your site should have a unique, descriptive title that includes your main keyword and ideally your location
  • Claim your Google Business Profile — if you haven't, do it now. It's free and drives significant local traffic
  • Fix your site speed — run your site through PageSpeed Insights (free tool from Google) and action the top 3 recommendations
  • Add a FAQ section to your key service pages — these often appear in Google's "People Also Ask" boxes, driving additional visibility

A simple content strategy that actually works

You don't need to publish 10 articles a week. One genuinely useful article per month, written for your actual target customer, is more valuable than 40 thin keyword-stuffed posts.

For each piece of content, start with a question: what is one thing my ideal customer wants to know that I can answer better than anyone else? Write that. Make it comprehensive. Update it when things change. That's a content strategy.

Over 12–18 months, a library of 15–20 genuinely useful articles on topics your customers search for will drive more enquiries than almost any paid channel — and compound over time rather than switching off when you stop spending.

Where to start if you're starting from scratch

  1. Month 1: Fix the technical basics. Fast site, proper titles, Google Business Profile
  2. Month 2–3: Write 2–3 cornerstone articles on your most important topics
  3. Month 4–6: Build supporting content around those core topics
  4. Ongoing: One new piece of content per month, update existing pages as needed

SEO is the slowest of all marketing channels to show results — typically 3–6 months before you see meaningful traffic — but it's also the one that pays back most reliably over time. Businesses that start today will be in a dramatically different position in 12 months than those who wait.
