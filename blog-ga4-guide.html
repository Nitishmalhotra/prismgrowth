What is GA4, exactly?

GA4 — Google Analytics 4 — is Google's current web analytics platform. It replaced Universal Analytics (UA) in July 2023, when Google switched it off for good. If you have a website, you almost certainly have GA4 installed on it already. But having it installed and actually understanding what it's telling you are two very different things.

Think of GA4 like a CCTV system for your website. It records everything: who visits, where they came from, what pages they looked at, how long they stayed, and whether they took any action (like filling in a contact form or making a purchase).

The most important thing to understand: GA4 doesn't just count page views. It tracks events — every click, scroll, form submission, video play, and button interaction on your site.

Why should your business care?

Here's a question: do you know which marketing channel brought in your last 10 customers? Was it Google Search? Instagram? A referral from another website? Or did they type your name directly into their browser?

If you can't answer that, you're making marketing decisions blind. GA4 answers it — if it's set up properly. Most businesses have GA4 running but have never configured it correctly, so they're collecting data without being able to make sense of it.

This matters because marketing budgets are finite. Every dollar you spend on a channel that isn't working is a dollar taken away from one that is.

What GA4 actually tracks

Out of the box, GA4 automatically tracks:

  • Where visitors come from — Google search, social media, email campaigns, direct visits
  • What pages they visit — and in what order
  • How long they stay — and when they leave
  • What device they use — mobile, desktop, tablet
  • Basic engagement signals — scrolls, outbound clicks, file downloads

With proper setup, you can also track:

  • Form submissions and lead captures
  • Phone number clicks
  • Video views
  • E-commerce transactions and revenue
  • Which marketing campaigns lead to actual sales

The gap most businesses have: GA4 is installed, but conversions (the actions that matter — enquiries, purchases, bookings) are not tracked. So they know how many people visited, but not how many converted.

The 3 most common GA4 mistakes we see

1. No conversion tracking

Your contact form is your most valuable page. If GA4 isn't recording every submission, you have no idea how well your site is turning visitors into leads. This is the single most common gap we find — and it takes about an hour to fix.

2. Not connecting GA4 to Google Ads

If you're running Google Ads and GA4 isn't linked to it, your ad campaigns are optimising for clicks — not actual business results. Linking the two means Google's algorithm learns from what actually matters: enquiries, purchases, and sales.

3. Looking at the wrong numbers

Most business owners open GA4 and look at total users. That number tells you very little on its own. What matters is conversion rate (what percentage of visitors take action) and channel performance (which traffic sources actually convert).

What to do next

If you've read this far and realised your GA4 might not be set up properly, here's where to start:

  1. Open GA4 and go to Reports → Conversions. If it's empty or only shows "purchase," your conversion tracking needs work.
  2. Go to Admin → Google Ads Links. If it's not connected, link it.
  3. Check Reports → Acquisition → Traffic Acquisition. This shows where your visitors come from. Make a note of your top 3 channels.
